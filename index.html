Solo production — concept to publish

The posts that perform best aren't the ones with the biggest production — they're the ones that find the exact right angle for a moment. BTC hitting $100K is a story about optimism, but the more interesting frame is the skeptics who were wrong: pile their quotes on screen, let reality do the work. The Burger King credit card analogy isn't about Burger King — it's about adoption curves and how every new payment rail felt absurd until it didn't.

My operating model is fully end-to-end: concept → script → asset curation → editing/motion → publishing strategy → post-level analytics. Every format decision — static vs. video, hook length, caption structure — is made with an eye on what the platform rewards at that moment.

The through-line

Simplify the idea until it's unavoidable, then time it right. The best crypto content doesn't explain — it reframes something the audience already half-believes.

This wasn't a standard campaign — it was a structured narrative experiment. The hypothesis: different narratives activate different investor profiles, and each platform has its own natural home for each angle.

I designed 10 creatives across 5 narrative pillars (Simplicity / No Stress, Diversification, Eligibility Criteria, Benchmark, Institutional Custody) and ran them simultaneously on IG and FB cores for cross-platform reads. X entered mid-flight as a tactical reach layer.

One constraint worth noting: Meta's ad system blocked creatives that referenced the fund directly (regulated financial product). I shifted the creative angle to the index rather than the fund — framing the campaign around the Nasdaq CME Crypto Index as a concept. The campaign was approved and ran cleanly.

Key finding

IG responds to emotional simplicity ("invest without stress") — more feminine audience, 45–54 peak. FB converts on rational thesis (diversification, custody, benchmark) — more intent-driven, older demographic in the South. These are different channels for different moments in the same funnel, not interchangeable.

X as a tactical layer

X used ~20% of Meta's budget (R$1.8K vs R$8.9K) and delivered ~46% of Meta's impressions at 2.3× cheaper CPM (R$2.40 vs R$5.60). CPC was comparable (R$0.36 vs R$0.32). The lesson: X is a reach and frequency channel for crypto-native audiences, not a conversion channel. Use it for technical/institutional narratives; keep emotional angles on IG.

03 / 05

Instagram content
revamp

Visual system · Hook optimization · Series structure · 2025

The Instagram content ecosystem had multiple recurring formats — weekly market analysis (Hash Insider), monthly CIO notes, educational series, product explainers, real-time commentary. But the visual system felt dated and engagement rates weren't matching what we achieved on X.

I led a full format refresh, working from diagnosis to execution. The goal wasn't to make things look nicer — it was to make each format work harder at the moment it appears in feed.

1
Visual consistency: Built a new template system aligned with brand guidelines. Each recurring format got its own visual signature — same brand, different personality per series.
2
Hook optimization: Rewrote the first 3 seconds of every video and the first slide of every carousel. The hook isn't decoration — it's the entire reason someone stops scrolling.
3
Series structure: Introduced recurring named formats (Hash Insider, Comece por Aqui) with predictable cadence. Predictability builds the habit of looking for the next one.
Why it matters beyond Instagram

Better creative systems improve both organic conversion and paid efficiency. When you have a strong template library, you can test more variations faster — which means paid campaigns learn faster and organic posts get refined faster. Creative infrastructure compounds.

05 / 05

AI content +
compliance infrastructure

Claude · MCP integrations · HubSpot · Multi-market · ~50% faster production

Most marketing teams use AI to generate one-off outputs. I took a different approach: build reusable infrastructure that codifies how the team thinks, writes, and reviews — so every output starts from a higher baseline.

Working across regulated markets (US SEC/FINRA, Europe, Brazil CVM) means every piece of content goes through compliance review. The bottleneck isn't creativity — it's the review cycle. I rebuilt the inputs so fewer things needed to be sent back.

🧠
Brand Voice Skill
Structured messaging architecture and writing constraints. Generates on-brand copy for any channel or market without manual style review.
✅
Compliance Pipeline
Region-aware guardrails for US, Europe, and Brazil. Flags regulatory risk before copy reaches legal review, reducing back-and-forth cycles.
📧
Subject Line Generator
A/B-ready subject line outputs with market-specific tone differences (US advisor vs. Brazil retail). Runs directly in HubSpot via MCP integration.
🎨
Brand Guidelines Skill
Design team's visual rules translated into structured AI instructions. Used for creative QA before assets go to compliance or publishing.
🔌
MCP / HubSpot Integration
Connected Claude to HubSpot via MCP protocol. Skills run in context — no copy-paste between tools, no context loss between steps.
📊
HTML Email QA
Automated quality check for HTML email renders — cross-client compatibility, dark mode, mobile layout — before anything goes to test send.
Impact

Lifecycle build cycle time reduced by ~50%. Fewer compliance review rounds. Consistent output quality across three markets and two languages — without a copywriter, a designer, or a compliance pre-checker on the team.

The broader point: this is what AI-enabled marketing looks like in practice — not using a chatbot to write captions, but building systems that make the whole team faster and safer. The skills library is maintained, versioned, and extended as new markets or product lines come online.
