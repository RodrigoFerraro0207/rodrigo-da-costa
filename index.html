Solo production — concept to publish

The posts that perform best aren't the ones with the biggest production — they're the ones that find the exact right angle for a moment. BTC hitting $100K is a story about optimism, but the more interesting frame is the skeptics who were wrong: pile their quotes on screen, let reality do the work. The Burger King credit card analogy isn't about Burger King — it's about adoption curves and how every new payment rail felt absurd until it didn't.

My operating model is fully end-to-end: concept → script → asset curation → editing/motion → publishing strategy → post-level analytics. Every format decision — static vs. video, hook length, caption structure — is made with an eye on what the platform rewards at that moment.

The through-line

Simplify the idea until it's unavoidable, then time it right. The best crypto content doesn't explain — it reframes something the audience already half-believes.

This wasn't a standard campaign — it was a structured narrative experiment. The hypothesis: different narratives activate different investor profiles, and each platform has its own natural home for each angle.

I designed 10 creatives across 5 narrative pillars (Simplicity / No Stress, Diversification, Eligibility Criteria, Benchmark, Institutional Custody) and ran them simultaneously on IG and FB cores for cross-platform reads. X entered mid-flight as a tactical reach layer.

One constraint worth noting: Meta's ad system blocked creatives that referenced the fund directly (regulated financial product). I shifted the creative angle to the index rather than the fund — framing the campaign around the Nasdaq CME Crypto Index as a concept. The campaign was approved and ran cleanly.

Key finding

IG responds to emotional simplicity ("invest without stress") — more feminine audience, 45–54 peak. FB converts on rational thesis (diversification, custody, benchmark) — more intent-driven, older demographic in the South. These are different channels for different moments in the same funnel, not interchangeable.

How to read this honestly

Causal attribution isn't possible in B2B2C distribution — too many variables. But the campaign was the only marketing input that changed materially during this period. BTC fell 13.4% and investor behavior didn't follow price action: the fund kept growing its shareholder base and maintained positive net flows on 19 of 24 trading days. Education content during volatility appears to function as a retention and conviction mechanism — not just an awareness tool.

→
I also ran a correlation analysis between daily BTC price variation and campaign traffic. Result: r = −0.06 — essentially zero. The highest-traffic day coincided with a −3.7% BTC move. Conclusion: pausing on down days is a brand call, not a media efficiency call. We documented this and built it into the 2026 playbook.

Editorial content
distribution system

Research → Social · CIO Notes · Primers · Outlook · Market Pulse · Inside the NCI · Multi-platform

Hashdex produces institutional-grade research content. My job is to take that content — built for sophisticated audiences — and translate it into formats that work across every channel, without losing the depth that makes it credible. When leadership recalibrates toward a more institutional focus, I recalibrate the entire distribution system with it.

The content ecosystem I distribute
Weekly
Inside the NCI
Weekly market analysis series — renamed from "Hash Insider" as a deliberate brand strategy decision to embed the Nasdaq CME Crypto Index name into Hashdex's flagship content format. The index we want to position as a global benchmark now lives in the name of our most consistent content touchpoint.
Monthly
Notes from the CIO + Research Insights
CIO-authored monthly letter on macro and crypto market dynamics. I contribute to narrative framing, then own the full distribution: social formats, email sequencing, channel-specific packaging across US, Europe, and Brazil.
Quarterly
Market Pulse
Quarterly market coverage report — translating dense performance data and macro context into digestible narratives for investors and advisors. Each edition gets a full distribution plan across social and lifecycle.
Annual + Deep dives
Outlook + Primers
Annual Outlook sets the investment thesis for the year ahead. Primers are deep-dive reports on specific ecosystem topics — DeFi, layer 2s, tokenization. Both require translating 20–40 page documents into social-native formats without flattening the argument.
How distribution works
1
Narrative contribution first. Before distribution, I work with the research and CIO teams on how the content should be framed for different audiences. The institutional version and the retail version of the same story are different stories.
2
Format decisions by channel. A Primer doesn't become a carousel — it becomes 3 carousels, a thread, an email sequence, and a webinar topic. Each format extracts a different layer of the same source material.
3
Visual system as brand consistency layer. Every recurring format has its own visual signature. When "Inside the NCI" appears in feed, it's instantly recognizable — predictability builds the audience habit of looking for the next one.
4
Calibrated to leadership direction. When the institutional focus sharpened in 2025, I recalibrated the entire system — tone, format choices, channel priority, and which content lines to amplify — to reflect where the business was going, not where it had been.
The strategic reframe

The "Inside the NCI" rename isn't a cosmetic change. It's a brand strategy decision: if you want to position an index as a global benchmark, you embed its name in the content that most consistently reaches your audience. Every edition of Inside the NCI reinforces the index as a reference point — not just a product feature.

AI infrastructure,

MCP integrations · Data pipeline · Content systems · Claude · X · Instagram · LinkedIn · HubSpot

Most people use AI to generate one-off outputs. I build systems. There are two layers to what I've built: a data pipeline that consolidates social and CRM data into automated dashboards, and a content production system that codifies how the team writes, reviews, and ships across regulated markets.

Neither required buying a third-party tool. Both required understanding how the underlying infrastructure works — and building on top of it directly.

Layer 1 — Social + CRM data pipeline
What this replaces

Tools like Sprout Social, Hootsuite Analytics, or Databox charge hundreds of dollars per month for a generic version of this. I built a fully customized version — with exactly the data I want, structured the way I need it — using MCPs I built myself inside the developer consoles of each platform.

1
Built apps inside the developer consoles of X, Instagram, LinkedIn, and HubSpot — establishing direct API access to the data I need without intermediary tools.
2
Created local MCPs for each platform that pull authenticated data on demand — engagement metrics, audience composition, reach, CRM list health — into a unified context.
3
HubSpot MCP goes beyond reporting — I use it to analyze audience lists directly, understanding which segments are receiving which communication journeys and how they're performing. Social behavior and CRM data in the same view.
4
Claude generates a consolidated HTML dashboard — customized, branded, and distributed to internal marketing channels monthly. What used to require hours of manual data pulling is now a single agent-driven workflow.
